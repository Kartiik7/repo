Introduction

  • Shahi Veg Biryani is a royal Indian delicacy that combines aromatic basmati rice with a rich vegetable and nut mixture, slow-cooked using the traditional dum pukht method.
  • ‘Shahi’ means ‘royal,’ and this dish truly lives up to its name with layers of ghee, whole spices, saffron, and creamy textures. Perfect for weddings, festivals, or any special occasion.

    • Origin of Shahi Biryani

  • Though they have Persian origin, biryani is famous throughout India, with each state having its variety of biryani. And for this, we need to be thankful to the Mughals as they bought this exotic dish to India.
  • Furthermore, the dish was further developed in Mughal kitchens. As a result, it became more and more popular in India.
  • It gets the name biriyani from the Persian words’ Birian‘, which means fried before cooking, and ‘Brinj,’ which means rice in Persia.
  • In India, each house has its own style of cooking biriyani since biryani is a dish that can make us feel comforted and indulgent at the same time.

    Shahi Veg Biryani Recipe

    Ingredients

    For Rice

    • 2 cups basmati rice
    • 4 cups water
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 2 green cardamoms
    • 3 cloves
    • 1 small cinnamon stick
    • 1 bay leaf

    For Vegetable Masala

    • 3 tbsp ghee
    • 1 cup onions, thinly sliced
    • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, peas, cauliflower)
    • 1 cup tomatoes, chopped
    • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 2 green chilies, slit
    • ½ cup yogurt (curd)
    • ¼ cup fresh cream
    • ½ tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp red chili powder
    • 1 tsp coriander powder
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves – a handful, chopped
    • Fresh mint leaves – a handful, chopped

    For Garnish & Flavor

    • 2 tbsp fried onions (birista)
    • 2 tbsp roasted cashews
    • 1 tbsp raisins
    • 1 tsp saffron strands soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk

    Instructions

    Step 1 – Prepare the Rice

    1. Wash and soak the basmati rice in water for 20–30 minutes.
    2. In a large pot, boil 4 cups water with salt, cardamoms, cloves, cinnamon, and bay leaf.
    3. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done (grains should still be firm).
    4. Drain the rice and set aside.

    Step 2 – Prepare the Vegetable Masala

    1. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottom pan.
    2. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
    3. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chilies; cook for a minute.
    4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
    5. Mix in turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
    6. Add mixed vegetables and cook for 3–4 minutes.
    7. Lower the heat, add yogurt and cream; cook until the gravy thickens.
    8. Stir in chopped coriander and mint leaves.

    Step 3 – Layer the Biryani

    1. In a heavy-bottom pot or handi, spread a layer of vegetable masala.
    2. Add a layer of rice over it.
    3. Sprinkle fried onions, roasted cashews, raisins, and saffron milk.
    4. Repeat the layers until all rice and masala are used, ending with a rice layer topped with garnish.

    Step 4 – Dum Cooking

    1. Seal the pot with aluminum foil or wheat dough.
    2. Cook on low heat for 20–25 minutes (dum method) so the flavors infuse.
    3. Switch off the heat and let it rest for 5 minutes before opening.

    Step 5 – Serve

    1. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork to avoid breaking the rice grains.
    2. Serve hot with raita, papad, and salad.
